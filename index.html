Module 1 — Why Charcoal?
The Case for Coal
Gas grills are convenient — but charcoal delivers something they simply can't: real smoke, a wide heat range, and those iconic caramelized flavors that define great barbecue.

Mastering charcoal takes a little patience. You'll learn to read the coals, manage airflow, and cook time and every session will sharpen those instincts. This course walks you through the Coalridge kettle grill step by step.

🌡️ High Heat Range

Charcoal reaches 700°F+, creating the Maillard reaction that browns and flavors meat at a level gas can't match.

💨 Smoke Flavor

Burning briquettes release compounds that infuse food with the unmistakable smokiness of real charcoal grilling.

🔥 Direct & Indirect

Coal arrangement lets you create hot and cool zones in a single cook — sear a steak and rest a chicken side by side.

🎯 Full Control

Vents, coal placement, and lid position all affect temperature — once you learn them, you command every cook.

Module 3 — Coal Readiness
Know When to Cook
Patience is the most underrated ingredient. Adding food too early wastes the cook — look for the ash layer and feel the heat before you place anything on the grate.
🔴
Just Lit — Not Ready
Coals are dark with visible flames. Internal temp 200–300°F. Wait 20–25 minutes.
🟠
Glowing — Almost
Orange glow visible, small flames. 400–500°F. Give it 5–10 more minutes.
⚪
Ashy White Edge — COOK NOW
Gray-white ash coating covers briquettes. 500–700°F. Peak heat. This is your window.
